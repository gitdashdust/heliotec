# Voice & Content Patterns

Extracted from live copy on heliotec.energy (2026-07-06). These are real examples,
not paraphrases — use them as reference for tone, not as a copyright-source to duplicate verbatim in other work.

## Tone
- Direct, confident, low-jargon. Sells outcomes ("Lower costs and lower carbon"), not features.
- Short declarative headlines, sentence case (not Title Case).
- Numbers do the persuading: stats are dropped in as their own bold visual moment
  (£2.3m, 150+, 2,000t, 5–25%) rather than buried in paragraphs.
- Second person, low pressure: "Curious about the savings potential in your sites?"
- Section eyebrows are short, all-caps, bracketed: `[ MULTI-SITE ENERGY MANAGEMENT ]`,
  `[ CAPABILITIES ]`, `[ PLATFORM ]`, `[ CASE STUDIES ]`, `[ HOW WE WORK ]`.
- Expandable/secondary links are prefixed with a `+`: `+ Learn more`, `+ Find your savings.`

## CTA pairing pattern
Primary + secondary CTA almost always ship together:
- "Try for free" (primary, lime pill button)
- "Schedule demo" (secondary, outline button)

Other CTAs seen: "Get your free energy review", "Request Callback", "Enquire today".

## Structural pattern for value props
Two-column "objective → value" framing is used for audience segmentation:

| OBJECTIVE (their words) | HELIOTEC'S VALUE (the answer) |
|---|---|
| "I want to cut my energy bills without the hassle…" | "See where you are overspending across every site, get a clear list of fixes, and let us handle the analysis." |

Reuse this pattern for new audience segments: quote the customer's internal
monologue, answer it directly, avoid restating the question.

## Case study format
`{result}%/£ savings` as a headline, one sentence of context, sector tag:
> **17.4% savings** — A airport hotel gained 17.4% on energy costs within 5 months, adding £35K to their bottom line. *(Leisure)*
